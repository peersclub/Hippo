2 · Client features — test on localhost:4000

Live AI research briefs OpenRouter · Claude Haiku 4.5

  1. Open the Hippo bubble (bottom-right) and ask What's driving BTC price today? tokens stream in live; the model tag anthropic/claude-haiku-4.5 shows in the card's top-right while streaming, not just at the endupdated — and the finished card carries real price, 12h change, funding stats and a sparkline
  2. Ask a concept question: What is a funding rate? a CONCEPT card — explanation prose, no market stats fabricated
  3. Ask the same question again. a CACHED BRIEF badge — answers are cached with a volatility-scaled TTL (the market-data layer also coalesces concurrent misses so a burst hits the exchange once)

Refresh in place new

  1. On any brief, tap ↻ REFRESH. the button holds a ↻ REFRESHING… pending state, then the same card updates in place with fresh data — it does not stack a new card below or leave the old one amber-stale

Numbers are retrieval, never generation

Every stat, sparkline and as-of timestamp comes straight from the live market-data feed (CCXT/Binance) — the model only writes prose. A hallucinated price cannot reach a stat cell.

No-advice guardrail

  1. Ask Should I buy BTC right now? a decline card — "◇ NO ADVICE — BY DESIGN" — pivoting to live facts instead of a recommendation. Enforced on the model's output, not just the prompt

Order preparation + venue handoff sim venue

  1. Type buy 0.05 BTC an order ticket — side, size, live price rows — with a "Review & confirm" CTA. Hippo prepares; the exchange confirms. Hippo never executes
  2. Confirm the ticket. lifecycle frames stream: awaiting confirm → filled, with a venue order id. If a confirm/cancel ever fails while online you now get an inline "couldn't reach the venue" message rather than silencenew

Portfolio

  1. Ask My positions & P&L a positions table drawn from real sim-venue state (empty state is honest — no fabricated rows)

Card interactions & multi-language

  1. ⧉ COPY puts clipboard text · ↗ SHARE opens the share overlay · 👍/👎 feedback (👎 asks one follow-up reason). all work in-card; feedback survives minimize/reopen
  2. Ask in Hindi or Hinglish: BTC ka funding rate kya hai? language detected; the answer follows it. SDK chrome also localizes (en / hi / hinglish / ar with RTL)

3 · Resilience & edge cases — the "never leaves you stuck" guarantees

These are the hardening cases — each has a deliberate, honest outcome instead of a dead spinner or silent failure.

Do thisExpected
Kill the AI service:
pkill -f uvicorn, then ask a research question		Never-500: you still get a truthful market-data-only brief + a "high market load" banner. Orders and prices keep working. (Restart with pnpm dev.)
Let a brief sit past its freshness windowThe as-of turns amber and REFRESH becomes the loudest control — stale data is declared, never silent.
Restart the gateway while the bubble is openThe SDK detects the dead stream and re-mints a fresh session automatically instead of hanging on "Reconnecting" forever.new
Block a user in the admin panel, then reload their demoSession mint returns 401 → the surface quietly disables (no error stack, no retry storm).new
Hit a partner's MAU quota (Bitrupt is at 3/3)Session mint returns 429 → a friendly "Hippo is busy this month" capacity notice with a long backoff, not an error.new
An order left un-actioned on the venueA per-ticket timeout emits an honest expired lifecycle frame so it can't hang in "awaiting confirm" forever.new
Redis outage (if REDIS_URL set)Answer cache falls back to in-memory with logged errors — it does not turn every answer into a decline card.new

Also under the hood: every cross-service call has a timeout, queued offline messages drain on a timer (not just on reconnect), unknown/forward-compat frames render a fallback instead of a stuck spinner, and a stalled stream finalizes honestly instead of a forever-blinking cursor.

Self-serve sandbox provisioning API

curl -X POST http://localhost:8794/v1/provision/sandbox \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"venueName":"My Exchange","email":"you@example.com","locales":["en"]}'

Returns a sandbox partner + a one-time claim URL for the JWT secret (15-min TTL, fetchable exactly once, never in any list). Rate-limited to 3/IP/hour. The new sandbox then appears in the admin Partners list ready to Activate.

5 · Partner portal — localhost:5176

The partner-facing plane (separate trust boundary from the operator admin): a partner sees only their own data — MAU vs quota, integration details (embed tag, secret rotation), plan view + change request, and their own audit trail. Operators invite a partner via a one-time claim link.
