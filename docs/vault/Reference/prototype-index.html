CHAPTER 01

The web terminal

Hippo docked beside the partner's chart as a 360px panel. The terminal, chart and order book belong to KoinBX; the conversation is ours. It never takes the trader hostage.

TRY THIS
  1. Tap the floating Ask Hippo button — confetti welcome → Show me more → data consent → Agree & start. The thread plays in.
  2. Tap a suggested query at the bottom of the panel: watch thinking → skeleton → streamed answer arrive live.
  3. Tap an order pill at the top — its full card expands in place. + New order takes plain-language intents.
  4. On the research brief: ↻ REFRESH updates the as-of time, ↗ SHARE shows the co-branded live card, 👍/👎 feeds the eval set.
  5. — minimizes the panel; wait ~5s for the amber market pulse. ⚙ opens settings — switch answers to Hinglish or preview RTL. Replay onboarding.

Why these states matter

The panel never takes the trader hostage: minimized, Hippo is one quiet amber tab and the partner's terminal is untouched — the co-brand promise made literal. The sheet opens over the chart, not instead of it; full-screen is opt-in for deep research. Same SDK, three postures.

CHAPTER 03

The order lifecycle

The Approach A seam, card by card: Hippo prepares, KoinBX confirms, and the thread always tells the truth about where the order stands. These are specification cards — each states the rule it enforces.

Why this seam is the product

Approach A means the confirm happens on KoinBX's screen, outside our surface — so the thread must stitch the journey back together: waiting, filled, partial, cancelled, expired. Every state is a server-driven card; the SDK only draws. If a trader ever has to leave the conversation to find out what happened to their order, this seam has failed.

CHAPTER 04

Edge states

Six states the SDK must ship with — empty, degraded, rejected, stale, offline. Every state answers three questions without being asked: what happened, what still works, what do I do next.

The shared principle

Every state answers three questions without the trader asking: what happened, what still works, and what do I do next. Degraded mode is the one with contract language behind it — the banner copy above is effectively the UI of the 99.5% SLA's graceful-degradation clause, so partners should see this screen during procurement, not discover it during an outage.
